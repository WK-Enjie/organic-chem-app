Introduction to Organic Chemistry

Foundations — what makes carbon special and how we classify organic compounds.

What is Organic Chemistry?

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds. Carbon is unique because it forms four covalent bonds and can bond to other carbon atoms to make chains, branches and rings.

💡

Key idea: Carbon compounds are everywhere — fuels (methane, petrol), materials (plastics), food (ethanol, vinegar).

🛢️ Crude Oil & Fuels

  • Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons separated by fractional distillation.
  • Fractions have competing uses: fuels and chemical feedstock.
  • Natural gas (mainly methane) and crude oil are non-renewable.
  • Biofuels (e.g. bioethanol from sugarcane) are renewable — more sustainable in terms of CO₂ emissions.

📋 Homologous Series

A homologous series is a family of compounds with:

  • The same general formula
  • Similar chemical properties
  • A gradation in physical properties
  • Each member differs by –CH₂–
  • The same functional group

🔀 Isomerism

Isomers have the same molecular formula but different structural formulae.

Example: C₄H₁₀ → butane (straight) or methylpropane (branched).

🔬 Saturated vs Unsaturated

Saturated: Only C–C single bonds (alkanes).

Unsaturated: Contains C=C double bond (alkenes).

🧪

Test: Aqueous bromine (brown) — decolourises with unsaturated, stays brown with saturated.

🧪 Weak Acid Properties

Carboxylic acids are weak acids — only partially ionise in water.

CH₃COOH ⇌ CH₃COO⁻ + H⁺

Typical acid reactions:

  • + Metal → salt + hydrogen ↑
  • + Base → salt + water
  • + Carbonate → salt + water + CO₂ ↑

Polymers

Large molecules built from repeating small units (monomers)

➕ Addition Polymerisation

Many alkene monomers join — C=C opens up → long chain with only C–C single bonds. No other product.

n CH₂=CH₂ →
high T & P –(CH₂–CH₂)–n

Ethene → Poly(ethene) — plastic bags, cling film.

💧 Condensation Polymerisation

Two different monomers join, releasing water each time.

Nylon (polyamide)

Dicarboxylic acid + diamine → –CONH– amide link. Uses: clothing, parachutes, fishing line.

Terylene (polyester)

Dicarboxylic acid + diol → –COO– ester link. Uses: clothing, curtains, sleeping bags.

🌍 Environmental Issues & Recycling

⚠️ Problems

  • Most plastics are non-biodegradable
  • Landfill waste — hundreds of years to break down
  • Burning releases toxic gases
  • Harms marine wildlife

♻️ Recycling

  • Physical: melt poly(ethene) → pellets → remoulded
  • Chemical: depolymerisation (hydrolysis of polyester with acid → monomers) or cracking waste into fuel
💡

Depolymerisation = breaking polymers back into monomers. E.g. polyester + H₂O (acid catalyst) → dicarboxylic acid + diol.
